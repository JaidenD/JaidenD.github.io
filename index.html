Lie Symmetry of PDEs

Research

Currently my research is to do with computing symmetries and conserved quantities of relativistic dynamics. From a symmetry standpoint, the PDEs governing relativistic dynamics are interesting because they can admit certain infinite dimensional families of symmetries. From a computational standpoint, they are interesting because modern symbolic manipulation programs destroy Lorentz invariance by "privileging" time.

Interests

My interest lie in the intersection of theoretical physics and mathematics, that is to say, I am interested in differential geometry, algebraic topology, representation theory, etc... Additionally, I am interested in Robert Hermann's program, the goal of which is to geometrically formulate ideas from engineering.
